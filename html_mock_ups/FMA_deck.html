Problem

The Core Problem

NZ retail traders are being influenced into high-risk offshore trading environments, but regulators lack behavioural insight into what happens next.

02
Current reality
  • Social media funnels NZ traders into offshore prop firms and brokers.
  • Harmful trading often occurs entirely outside the NZ regulatory perimeter.
  • Existing reporting frameworks provide little or no behavioural detail.
  • FMA sees complaints and outcomes, but not the behavioural journey.

The result: a large, data-poor blind spot where most high-risk behaviour now occurs.

Insights

What We Can Learn From This Window

Even a modest slice of NZ traders provides rich insight into risk behaviour, harm patterns, and systemic exposure.

05
Key insights
  • How NZ traders actually behave under stress and volatility.
  • Risk levels by cohort (leverage, instrument, session time).
  • Stop-loss behaviour as a proxy for discipline and education.
  • Time-to-failure and recurring blow-up patterns.
  • Harm clustering where many users make the same mistake together.

None of this is currently visible to the FMA through standard reporting.

Scoring

Behavioural Risk Scoring (PilotScore v1)

PilotScore gives the FMA a single behavioural lens to quantify retail trading harm and risk across NZ cohorts.

06
PilotScore measures
  • Stability and consistency of trading behaviour.
  • Drawdown patterns and recovery discipline.
  • Risk management markers (position sizing, stop placement).
  • Volatility sensitivity and overexposure tendencies.
Insights for the FMA
  • Average NZ PilotScore across the sample.
  • Score distribution by cohort (age, instrument, product type).
  • Correlation between low scores and realised losses.

Influence

Influence Pattern Inference (Without Monitoring Influencers)

We detect social-driven risk via trading patterns, not by scraping or monitoring offshore content.

07
Behavioural signatures of influence
  • Multiple NZ traders entering similar positions within tight time windows.
  • New cohorts activating around specific assets or strategies.
  • Rush into the same instrument at unusual hours.
  • Volatility events that trigger correlated, leveraged positions.

These signatures reveal likely influence and herd behaviour without any content surveillance.

Offshore Funnels

Offshore Prop Trading Funnel Analysis

Using the sample we can analyse how NZ traders behave inside offshore prop and high-risk environments, even without full market coverage.

08
Key measures (within the sample)
  • Proportion of integrated users trading offshore prop products.
  • Time-to-failure: how long until a typical account blows up.
  • Frequency of repeated account blow-ups by the same individuals.
  • Behavioural signatures that reliably predict loss (e.g., leverage spikes, no stop-loss).

These indicators provide a first view of NZ leakage into offshore high-risk markets – SupTech 101.

Roadmap

SupTech Sandbox Proposal (3-Phase Pilot)

A realistic, slow-burn approach that starts with voluntary data and evolves towards broader industry engagement.

12

Phase 1 – Voluntary Data Study

Pilot shares anonymised behavioural datasets from integrated NZ users. Cambridge validates methodology. FMA reviews early indicators.

Phase 2 – Harm Taxonomy

Joint development of a harm taxonomy and behavioural risk categories aligned with FMA’s consumer outcomes and policy needs.

Phase 3 – Broker Engagement

Once evidence is established, invite brokers to participate voluntarily, test tools, and explore integration paths.

No mandates. No forced integrations. A SupTech sandbox that grows as trust and evidence build.

Positioning

What Pilot Is Not Claiming

Clear boundaries are important. We set expectations realistically to build trust with the FMA.

13
We are not claiming:
  • To monitor or moderate offshore influencer content.
  • To cover every NZ trader or every platform.
  • To replace existing supervisory frameworks.
  • To eliminate offshore harm overnight.
  • To unilaterally enforce suitability standards.
We are offering to:
  • Provide behavioural insight that does not exist today.
  • Quantify harm and risk using a consistent metric.
  • Prototype SupTech tools that can scale if valuable.
  • Support FMA policy and consumer guidance with evidence.

Honest, constrained claims increase credibility and make the SupTech pilot easier to approve.
