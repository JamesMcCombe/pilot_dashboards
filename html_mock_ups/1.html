Intro

A SupTech Prototype for Understanding Retail Trading Harm in New Zealand

Pilot proposes a joint SupTech initiative with the FMA and Cambridge University’s SupTech Lab to reveal behavioural risk patterns in NZ retail trading that are currently invisible.

01
What this prototype does
  • Creates a safe, voluntary “indicator dataset” for NZ retail trading behaviour.
  • Focuses on offshore, high-risk funnels where harm is most likely but least visible.
  • Anchors analysis around a single behavioural lens: PilotScore.
Why start here
  • Regulators currently see complaints and outcomes, not the behavioural journey.
  • Even a small sample of NZ traders reveals patterns and harm signatures.
  • This is a research-first SupTech pilot, not a new set of rules.

The goal of this slide: orient FMA and Cambridge to the idea that insight, not coverage, is the first step.
