Slide 2

New Zealand retail traders are leaking into offshore high-risk trading environments

…but regulators have no behavioural insight into what happens next.

02

Most harmful retail trading activity now occurs outside NZ’s regulatory perimeter:

  • Foreign prop firms
  • Offshore brokers
  • Social-media trading funnels
  • Influencer-driven schemes

Because this behaviour happens offshore, existing NZ frameworks cannot see the harm, cannot quantify it, and cannot model it.

This prototype aims to give the FMA the first-ever indicator dataset to understand this.

Slide 3

What We Need From the FMA

03

We ask the FMA to agree to two foundational statements:

  1. NZ retail traders are leaking into unregulated offshore trading funnels.
  2. Insight into their behaviour is required to design effective harm-reduction policy.

These two statements form the basis for a SupTech research program. They do not require regulatory change, only FMA acknowledgment that this is a priority area of research.

Once this foundation is agreed, a Cambridge-aligned SupTech prototype becomes viable.

Slide 4

What We CAN Observe Today

A small but meaningful behavioural window exists.

04

We are not proposing universal surveillance. We are proposing indicator datasets, the standard approach in SupTech.

Through opt-in integrations with tools widely used by NZ traders — NinjaTrader, MT4/MT5, and Interactive Brokers APIs — Pilot can already observe:

  • Trade timing
  • Leverage usage
  • Stop-loss behaviour
  • Volatility exposure
  • Account blowup patterns
  • Clustering of user behaviour

Even a small dataset reveals patterns that are invisible today.

Slide 5

What We Can Learn From This Window

Behavioural harm insights the FMA does not currently have.

05

From even a small slice of NZ traders, we can generate:

  1. How New Zealand traders actually behave
    • Overtrading
    • News chasing
    • Revenge trading
  2. Risk levels and leverage habits
    • Excessive leverage vs normalised patterns
  3. Stop-loss usage and discipline
    • A key marker of harm and education level
  4. Patterns of account destruction
    • Time-to-failure signatures
    • Volatility-driven liquidation patterns
  5. Harm clustering
    • Many users making the same mistake at the same time

All of this is insight NZ does not receive from brokers today.

Slide 7

Influence Pattern Inference (without monitoring influencers)

Detecting social-driven risk behaviour — without accessing content.

07

We do not need to see influencers. We infer influence through actionable behavioural signatures:

  • When NZ users cluster into the same trades
  • When new cohorts activate at identical times
  • When traders rush into the same instrument
  • When unusual trading hours spike
  • When volatility events trigger mass behaviour

This is social herd detection. It reveals the presence of influence even when we cannot see the content.

Regulators have never had access to this type of insight.

Slide 12

SupTech Sandbox Proposal (3-Phase Pilot)

12

Phase 1 — Voluntary Data Study (Now)

  • Pilot provides anonymised behavioural dataset from NZ users
  • Cambridge validates methodology
  • FMA observes indicators

Phase 2 — Harm Taxonomy Development (6–12 months)

  • Behavioural risk categories
  • Influence patterns
  • Loss archetypes
  • Policy alignment

Phase 3 — Broker Engagement (Future)

  • Only once evidence is established
  • Voluntary broker participation
  • Suitability tools
  • Consumer protection enhancements

This is a slow, safe, realistic path that requires no regulation and no broker integration to begin.
